- Rare, extreme events (“black swans”) have far more impact on history, markets, and life than frequent, predictable events.
    
- Human thinking is biased toward stories and past data, so we systematically underestimate the role of randomness and tail events.
    
- Prefer “robustness” and “antifragility” over fragile over‑optimization: build systems that survive or benefit from shocks instead of trying to predict them.
    
- Use heuristics like keeping optionality, simplifying exposure, and avoiding over‑reliance on complex models, especially in finance or policy.
